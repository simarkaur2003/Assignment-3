KitKat

KitKat is a chocolate-covered wafer bar that was created by Rowntree's of York, England in 1935. It is now produced globally by Nestle, which acquired Rowntree in 1988. The classic four-finger KitKat is the most popular version of the candy bar, but there are many other flavors and sizes available.

Did you know?

  • KitKat is known as Kit in the United States and Canada.
  • It takes about 10 hours to produce a KitKat bar.
  • The name KitKat is thought to have been inspired by a type of mutton pie that was popular in the 18th century.
  • The original slogan for KitKat was "Treat yourself to a big hearty CHUNK of milk chocolate!"

Our History

KitKat was first created in the United Kingdom in 1935 as Rowntree's Chocolate Crisp. The chocolate-covered wafer bar was renamed KitKat Chocolate Crisp in 1937 and later shortened to just KitKat in 1949. Today, KitKat is sold in over 80 countries and has become a beloved treat around the world.
